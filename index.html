Background

I'm a quantitative finance graduate student at Fordham University's Gabelli School of Business. My work sits at the intersection of financial mathematics, statistical computing, and production-grade software engineering.

I design and implement end-to-end quantitative systems — from stochastic calculus-based options pricing engines (Black-Scholes PDE, Crank-Nicolson finite difference, PSOR/LCP for early exercise) to full market-risk analytics pipelines (multi-methodology VaR, EWMA covariance, Cholesky-based Monte Carlo, Kupiec & Christoffersen backtesting).

Prior to Fordham, I completed a BS in Economics with a minor in Finance at Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies in Mumbai, and interned at Jain Vinay & Associates, a chartered accountancy firm, where I managed financial data for 500+ monthly transactions and ensured 100% compliance in financial reporting accuracy.

Quantitative Risk Analytics — VaR Framework

End-to-end market risk analytics pipeline built in Python for a $10M multi-asset portfolio (10 assets). Implements seven distinct VaR methodologies, full backtesting suite, EWMA covariance modelling, component risk decomposition, and historical crisis stress testing.

  • Backtested across 4,300+ trading days with Kupiec POF test and Christoffersen independence test; validated exception clustering and model calibration
  • Stress tested across GFC 2008, COVID-19 crash, and rate-shock regimes — reporting ES at 97.5% for each crisis window

Quantitative Option Pricing Engine — OptionLab

Full-stack options analytics platform supporting European and American vanilla options. Combines analytic Black–Scholes with a finite-difference PDE solver (Crank-Nicolson θ-scheme) and is deployed as an interactive live application at optionlab.streamlit.app.

  • Black–Scholes closed-form pricing for European options; PSOR-based Linear Complementarity Problem (LCP) solver for American early-exercise with Rannacher smoothing to stabilise payoff discontinuities
  • Newton–bisection hybrid implied volatility solver; full Greeks computation (Δ, Γ, Θ, Vega, Rho) with interactive surface plots
  • Interactive visualisations: payoff vs. value analysis, Greeks curves, PDE heatmaps, early-exercise boundaries, and price surfaces via Plotly
  • Deployed on Streamlit Cloud — fully live and publicly accessible; built with NumPy, Pandas, SciPy, and Plotly
