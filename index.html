Who I Am

I'm a graduate student in Quantitative Finance at Fordham University's Gabelli School of Business, with a background in Economics from Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies in Mumbai, India. My academic journey has been shaped by a deep curiosity for the mathematics that underpins financial markets — stochastic processes, numerical methods, and statistical inference.

I enjoy building things end-to-end: taking a mathematical framework — whether it's the Black-Scholes PDE, EWMA covariance estimation, or a Cholesky-decomposed Monte Carlo simulation — and turning it into a clean, working, production-quality system. My VaR Framework and OptionLab app are reflections of this philosophy.

Before Fordham, I interned at Jain Vinay & Associates in Mumbai, where I worked on financial statements, audit documentation, and data reconciliation — experience that gave me appreciation for the precision financial work demands.

Quantitative Risk Analytics — VaR Framework

End-to-end market risk analytics pipeline built in Python for a multi-asset portfolio. Implements seven distinct VaR methodologies, a full backtesting suite under Kupiec and Christoffersen tests, EWMA covariance modelling, component risk decomposition, and historical crisis stress testing.

  • Implemented 7 VaR methodologies: Historical Simulation, Bootstrap HS, EWMA-weighted, Volatility-weighted HS, Correlation-adjusted HS, Delta-Normal (Parametric), and Monte Carlo with Cholesky decomposition
  • MLE-estimated EWMA λ, covariance series via EWMA, Cholesky decomposition for multivariate normal MC simulation, and delta-normal component VaR per asset
  • Backtested across 4,300+ trading days with Kupiec POF test and Christoffersen independence test; validated exception clustering and model calibration
  • Historical stress testing across GFC 2008, COVID-19 crash, and rate-shock regimes — reporting Expected Shortfall at 97.5% for each crisis window

Quantitative Option Pricing Engine — OptionLab

Full-stack options analytics platform supporting European and American vanilla options. Combines analytic Black–Scholes with a finite-difference PDE solver (Crank-Nicolson θ-scheme) deployed as an interactive analytics application.

  • Black–Scholes closed-form pricing for European options; PSOR-based LCP solver for American early-exercise with Rannacher smoothing to stabilise payoff discontinuities
  • Newton–bisection hybrid implied volatility solver; full Greeks (Δ, Γ, Θ, Vega, Rho) with interactive surface plots via Plotly
  • Interactive visualisations: payoff vs. value analysis, Greeks curves, PDE heatmaps, early-exercise boundaries; built with NumPy, Pandas, SciPy, Plotly

About the Programme
The MS in Quantitative Finance at Fordham's Gabelli School is a rigorous, mathematically intensive programme designed to train students in the quantitative methods that underpin modern financial markets.
